About Me

I am a photographer drawn to the quiet moments — the light shifting over a landscape, the small details most people walk past, the stillness between things happening. My work spans landscapes, portraits, florals, food, vehicles, and wildlife, but the thread running through all of it is the same: I want to show the world the way it looks when you slow down enough to really see it.

A trip through the South Island of New Zealand changed the way I think about photography. I stopped planning shots and started reacting to what was in front of me — pulling over on gravel roads, waiting for the light to do something worth capturing. That approach stuck. No presets, no overthinking. Just patience and the willingness to stay a little longer than I think I need to.

When I am not behind a camera, I am probably thinking about where to point one next.
